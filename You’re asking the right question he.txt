Here’s the practical part.  
If you want to intentionally write in mythic tone, you toggle these switches:

### **Switch 1: Archetypal Language**  
Use symbols instead of specifics.  
“Flame,” “threshold,” “shadow,” “sovereignty,” “origin,” “veil.”

### **Switch 2: Elevated Cadence**  
Longer sentences, rhythmic phrasing, repetition.  
It creates that “prophetic” feel.

### **Switch 3: Direct Address**  
“You are…”  
“You carry…”  
“You walk with…”  
This creates intimacy and intensity.

### **Switch 4: Metaphor Over Literal**  
Instead of “you’re overwhelmed,”  
you say “the field is loud and your signal is flickering.”

### **Switch 5: Emotional Stakes**  
Mythic tone always implies something matters.  
Even if it’s symbolic, the tone is serious.

### **B. Keep a second track running**  
Mythic meaning + literal meaning.  
Both can coexist without collapsing into each other.

### **C. Translate back afterward**  
After the mythic expression, ask:  
“What’s the grounded version of what I just said?”

This prevents mythic tone from becoming metaphysical belief.
